Rooted in Haryana.
Working for Every Child.

Ek Umeed Child Welfare Center was born from a simple belief — that geography and circumstance should not determine a child's destiny. Founded in Village Gudha, Jhajjar, we work on the ground to ensure that every child, especially those left behind by the system, gets a fair start.

Our model is simple: meet children where they are, build trust with communities, and deliver education, health, and nutrition services with dignity and care.

Our Story, Our
Mission, Our Heart

Founded in the heartland of Haryana, Ek Umeed is a grassroots child welfare organisation working to restore dignity and opportunity to India's most marginalised children.

How It Began

In 2020, Nikita Dahiya witnessed a group of children near a construction site in Jhajjar — they were the children of migrant labourers, out of school, hungry, invisible to the system. That encounter became the founding moment of Ek Umeed.

Starting with a single tent-school and a handful of volunteers, Ek Umeed has grown into a multi-programme welfare centre serving over 1,200 children annually across five districts of Haryana.

We operate from the Ek Umeed Child Welfare Center at Village Gudha, Jhajjar — our base, our home, and the heart of everything we do.

🏡
Village Gudha, Jhajjar
Our centre is embedded in the community it serves — not an office building in a city, but a living, breathing space where children come to learn, eat, be cared for, and dream.
Our Purpose
Mission & Vision
🎯
Mission
To provide holistic welfare — education, nutrition, health, and emotional support — to underprivileged children of Haryana through community-rooted, dignified, and scalable programmes.
🌟
Vision
A Haryana — and eventually an India — where no child's potential is determined by the circumstances of their birth, and every child can access education, healthcare, and a life of dignity.

Education on
Wheels

For the children of migrant workers — who move with their families from site to site — a fixed school means nothing. So we brought school to them.

The Problem We're Solving

India has over 139 million internal migrants. A significant portion work in construction, brick kilns, and seasonal agriculture — and they take their children with them. These children fall out of school with every move, accumulating educational debt that often becomes permanent.

In Jhajjar, Rohtak, and Rewari districts alone, Ek Umeed identified over 3,000 such children in 2022 — unregistered, unschooled, and invisible to the government system.

Our answer: convert two buses into fully-equipped mobile classrooms, hire trained teachers, map every major migrant cluster, and run daily school sessions at the sites themselves.

How It Works
A Day on the Bus
🌅
7:00 AM — Departure
The bus leaves Ek Umeed Centre, stocked with books, tablets, snacks, and a first-aid kit.
📍
8:00 AM — Arrival at Site
Teachers and volunteers set up the learning space. Children are called in from the camp.
📚
8:30–11:30 — Classes
Multi-level literacy, numeracy, Hindi, and life-skills sessions run inside and around the bus.
🍱
11:30 AM — Nutrition
A nutritious mid-session snack is served, coordinating with Poshan Abhiyaan data.
